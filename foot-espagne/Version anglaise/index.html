Since May 24th, Spain is officially at the top of the European football. It now holds the four major titles a European nation can hope for: the national team won both the World Cup (2010) and the European Championship (2008), Sevilla FC won the Europa League on May 14th and Real Madrid won its 10th Champions League title on May 24th in a 100% Spanish final against Atlético Madrid.

Such a domination only happened twice in football history: in 2012, Spain had already held the 4 titles... for a dozen days, before FC Barcelona was defeated by Chelsea in the Champions League final. But the only nation which succeeded to finish a year with these four titles is Germany in 1975. That year, after the national team's victories in the European Championship (1972) and the World Cup (1974), Bayern Munich won the Champion Clubs' Cup and Borussia Mönchengladbach got the UEFA Cup. Spain now has a chance to do the same in the next few weeks, during the World Cup in Brazil.
